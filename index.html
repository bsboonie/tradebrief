Welcome to TradeBrief

Your personal options trading morning briefing — built around your rules, your budget, and your watchlist.

What is TradeBrief?

TradeBrief is a personal trading intelligence tool designed for retail options traders who buy debit spreads, long calls, and long puts. Every morning before the market opens, TradeBrief gives you a clear picture of your watchlist stocks — their trend direction, momentum, and whether conditions are right for a trade.

Think of it as your pre-market routine, automated. Instead of opening multiple browser tabs and manually checking charts, TradeBrief pulls it all into one clean dashboard.

🎯
Defined Risk Only
Every trade you log has a known max loss before you enter. You can never lose more than you paid. No surprises.
📈
Trend-Based Signals
CALLS, PUTS, or WAIT — signals are generated from RSI and EMA analysis, the same indicators you use on TradingView.
💡
Smart Take Profit
TradeBrief doesn't just watch your profit % — it monitors the trend too, so it tells you to hold when the stock is still climbing.

What TradeBrief Does NOT Do

⚠️ TradeBrief is a decision-support tool — not financial advice. It helps you organize information and apply your own rules consistently. Always do your own research and verify data on Tradier before placing any trade.

TradeBrief does not support naked calls, cash-secured puts, or any undefined-risk strategy. It is built exclusively for traders who want to know their maximum loss before entering.

Your Daily Workflow

TradeBrief is designed around a simple morning routine. Here's how to use it from start to finish.

1
Open TradeBrief — Data Loads Automatically

When you open the app, it automatically fetches current price data for every stock on your watchlist. No button needed. Each card shows the ticker with its price, RSI, EMA trend, and directional signal — all collapsed for a quick scan.

2
Scan the Briefing Cards

Look at the left border color and the signal badge on each card. Green border + CALLS = bullish setup. Red border + PUTS = bearish. Amber = wait. Check the RSI number — if it's in the 40–60 range and the EMAs confirm the direction, that's your best candidate.

Green border + CALLS — Price above both EMAs, RSI healthy, uptrend confirmed
Red border + PUTS — Price below both EMAs, RSI not oversold, downtrend confirmed
Amber border + WAIT — Mixed signals, overbought, or oversold without confirmation
3
Expand a Card to Dig Deeper

Tap the ∨ chevron on any card to expand it. You'll see the TradingView live chart and three key stat blocks with explanations. Use this to confirm the signal before going to Tradier.

4
Pull the Option Chain from Tradier

Open Tradier, navigate to the option chain for your stock. Take screenshots of each expiration you want to analyze (aim for 21–35 days out). Come back to this chat, upload the screenshots, and type cvsit. Claude will extract the data as a CSV.

5
Run the Options Spreadsheet

Paste the CSV into your Google Sheets Options Calculator (RAW_FIDELITY tab). Update the CONTROLS tab with the current stock price, ticker, and your budget. The scanner runs automatically and shows you the Top 3 call spreads and put spreads ranked by quality.

6
Place the Trade on Tradier

Review the GOOD-rated spreads. Look at R/R ratio (minimum 1.5x), cost vs your budget, and break-even vs expected move. Place your trade on Tradier. Then come back and log it in TradeBrief's Trades tab.

7
Monitor with the Open Positions Dashboard

Your open trade appears in the Briefing tab below the stock cards. Watch the status badge. When it says 🎯 TAKE PROFIT or 🔥 CLOSE NOW — go to Tradier and close the position. Then come back and log the close in the Trades tab.

Tips for Best Results

✅ Check TradeBrief every morning before market open (9:00–9:25 AM ET)
✅ Only trade stocks where RSI is between 35–65 — avoid extremes unless you know what you're doing
✅ Always confirm the signal with the TradingView chart before pulling the option chain

Options & Trading Glossary

Every term you'll encounter in TradeBrief — explained in plain English, with context for why it matters to your trading.

Call Option
The right to BUY 100 shares of a stock at a specific price (the strike) before a specific date (expiration). You profit when the stock goes UP.
💡 Why it matters: Calls let you control 100 shares with a fraction of the capital. Your max loss is limited to what you paid.
Put Option
The right to SELL 100 shares of a stock at a specific price before expiration. You profit when the stock goes DOWN.
💡 Why it matters: Puts let you profit from falling stocks without shorting — with defined, limited risk.
Long Call
Buying a call option outright with no ceiling on profit. If the stock rockets to the moon, you capture all of it. Cost is higher than a spread.
💡 Why it matters: Best when you have very strong conviction. Higher cost but unlimited upside potential.
Call Spread (Debit Spread)
Buying a call at one strike AND selling a call at a higher strike. Caps your max profit but significantly reduces your cost. Both strikes must expire.
💡 Why it matters: Better R/R ratio than a long call. Cheaper entry means you can be wrong and still survive. TradeBrief defaults to this strategy.
Long Put
Buying a put option outright. Profits when the stock falls. Unlimited profit potential (down to zero). Higher cost than a put spread.
💡 Why it matters: Use when you have strong bearish conviction and want to maximize downside profit.
Put Spread
Buying a put at one strike AND selling a put at a lower strike. Caps profit but reduces cost significantly.
💡 Why it matters: Defined risk on both sides. Cheaper than a long put and still benefits from downward moves.
Strike Price
The price at which you have the right to buy (call) or sell (put) shares. Chosen when you enter the trade.
💡 Why it matters: Choosing the right strike determines your break-even, your cost, and your probability of profit.
Expiration Date (DTE)
The date the option contract expires and becomes worthless if not exercised. DTE = Days To Expiration.
💡 Why it matters: Options lose value as expiration approaches (time decay). TradeBrief targets 21–35 DTE for best balance of cost and time.
Premium
The price you pay to buy an option. This is your total risk — the most you can lose.
💡 Why it matters: Always know your premium before entering. If you can't afford to lose it, don't trade it.
Break-Even
The stock price at which your trade makes exactly $0 profit. For a call spread: Buy Strike + Net Cost per share.
💡 Why it matters: Your break-even must be within the stock's expected move range. TradeBrief checks this automatically.
R/R (Reward-to-Risk Ratio)
Max Profit divided by Max Loss. A 2.0 R/R means you could make $200 for every $100 risked.
💡 Why it matters: TradeBrief requires a minimum 1.5 R/R. Trades below this aren't worth the risk mathematically.
PoP (Probability of Profit)
The estimated chance your trade will be profitable at expiration, derived from the option's delta.
💡 Why it matters: Higher PoP = more likely to win but usually lower payout. TradeBrief minimum is 30% PoP.
Delta
How much the option price moves for every $1 move in the stock. A delta of 0.40 means the option gains $0.40 when the stock gains $1.
💡 Why it matters: Delta is also a rough probability estimate. TradeBrief targets 0.15–0.70 delta for balanced trades.
IV (Implied Volatility)
The market's expectation of how much a stock will move. High IV = expensive options. Low IV = cheap options.
💡 Why it matters: Buying options when IV is very high means you're overpaying. TradeBrief warns you when IV rank is elevated.
IV Crush
After a major event (like earnings), IV drops sharply — causing option prices to collapse even if the stock moved your way.
💡 Why it matters: This is why TradeBrief flags any trade where earnings falls before expiration as RISKY. IV crush can wipe out your profit.
RSI (Relative Strength Index)
A momentum indicator from 0–100. Above 70 = overbought (due for a pullback). Below 30 = oversold (due for a bounce). Sweet spot: 40–60.
💡 Why it matters: RSI tells you if a stock is extended. Buying calls on an RSI of 80 is buying at the top. TradeBrief uses RSI 14-period.
EMA (Exponential Moving Average)
A moving average that gives more weight to recent prices. EMA 8 reacts fast. EMA 21 is slower and smoother.
💡 Why it matters: When price is above both EMAs and EMA 8 is above EMA 21, that's a confirmed uptrend — the ideal entry for calls.
EMA Crossover
When the fast EMA (8) crosses above or below the slow EMA (21). A bullish crossover signals upward momentum. A bearish crossover signals downward momentum.
💡 Why it matters: TradeBrief uses EMA alignment (both EMAs in the same direction, price above/below both) to confirm trend before signaling CALLS or PUTS.
Expected Move
How far the market expects a stock to move by expiration, calculated from the ATM option prices.
💡 Why it matters: Your break-even must be inside the expected move or the trade is unlikely to profit. TradeBrief checks this automatically.
Time Decay (Theta)
Options lose value every day just from the passage of time, even if the stock doesn't move. This is theta decay.
💡 Why it matters: The closer to expiration, the faster the decay. This is why you close winning trades early instead of holding to expiration.
Open Interest
The total number of active contracts at a given strike. High open interest = liquid, easy to trade. Low open interest = harder to fill.
💡 Why it matters: Always check open interest before trading. Strikes with less than 100 open interest may have wide bid/ask spreads that cost you money.
Bid/Ask Spread
The difference between what buyers will pay (bid) and what sellers want (ask). You typically buy at the ask and sell at the bid.
💡 Why it matters: Wide bid/ask spreads eat into your profit. Always try to fill near the midpoint price. Liquid stocks have tighter spreads.
Realized P&L
Profit or loss from trades you have already closed. This is real money — in or out of your account.
💡 Why it matters: The only P&L that counts. Unrealized P&L can disappear before you close.
Unrealized P&L
The current paper profit or loss on open positions. What you WOULD make if you closed right now.
💡 Why it matters: Watch this closely. A trade showing +120% unrealized can reverse fast. When the signal weakens, lock it in.

What is TradeBrief?

TradeBrief is a personal trading intelligence tool designed for retail options traders who buy debit spreads, long calls, and long puts. It gives you a clear picture of watchlist stocks — their trend direction, momentum, and whether conditions are right for a trade.

Think of it as your pre-market routine, automated. Instead of opening multiple browser tabs and manually checking charts, TradeBrief pulls it all into one clean dashboard.

Three Tools, One Workflow
TradeBrief Gate 1 — Scanner
Scans 127 liquid, optionable stocks across 12 sectors. Ranks by confidence score. Shows the top candidates worth investigating.
TradeBrief Gate 2 — Thesis Builder
Build your trade thesis directly in the briefing card. AI scores your thesis 0–10 and adds it to your conviction score. Select a ticker to send it to TradeSheet with directional bias pre-loaded.
TradeSheet Gate 3 — Option Analyzer
Analyze spreads and long options. Ticker and directional bias flow in from TradeJournal. Auto-populates fields where possible. TradeLog lives here — log your trade after placing it in Tradier.
What TradeBrief Does NOT Do
⚠️ TradeBrief is a decision-support tool — not financial advice. It helps you organize information and apply your own rules consistently. Always do your own research and verify data on Tradier before placing any trade.

TradeBrief does not support naked calls, cash-secured puts, or any undefined-risk strategy. It is built exclusively for traders who want to know their maximum loss before entering.

The Three-Gate Workflow

TradeBrief is designed around a simple morning routine. Each panel is a gate — but none of them lock you out. You can access any panel directly at any time. The gates are guides, not guards.

1
Open TradeBrief — Scan the Universe

Hit Scan Now. TradeBrief pulls quotes and history for 127 stocks across 12 sectors. Top candidates are ranked by confidence score (0–100). Review the top results. When you find one worth investigating, hit Select.

2
TradeJournal — Build Your Thesis

The selected ticker flows into TradeJournal automatically. News auto-fetches. ATR auto-calculates. Work through the four thesis questions with AI coaching. Resolve to BULLISH or BEARISH. When your thesis score is solid, move to TradeSheet.

3
TradeSheet — Analyze the Option

Ticker pre-loads. Directional bias badge reminds you what the thesis said. Review the top spreads or long options. Check R/R, cost vs budget, break-even vs expected move. When you're ready, place the trade on Tradier.

4
Log the Trade in TradeLog

After placing in Tradier, come back to TradeSheet and hit Log This Trade. Fill in the details. TradeLog tracks your open and closed positions and calculates your running P&L.

Tips
✅ Run TradeBrief every morning before market open (9:00–9:25 AM ET) for the freshest data.
✅ If you placed an order after hours, review it pre-market before it executes at open.
✅ Target 3–4 weeks minimum on expiration for long options. Theta eats cheap short-dated contracts fast.
✅ The confidence score is a filter, not a guarantee. 52–55% directional accuracy is the professional ceiling for technical scanning.
